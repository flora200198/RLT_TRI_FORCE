Tamil Nadu Uniformed Services Recruitment Board (TNUSRB) - Sub Inspector ( Technical )
1. Selection Process for TNUSRB - Sub Inspector ( Technical )
The Selection Process for TNUSRB – Sub Inspector ( Technical ) are :-
  1. Written Examination
  2. Viva-Voice
  3. Medical Test
2. Educational Qualification for TNUSRB - Sub Inspector ( Technical )
  • Minimum a second class Diploma in Electronics and Communication Engineering awarded by the State Board of Technical Education, Tamil Nadu
  • A Degree (B.E/B.Tech) in Electronics and Communication Engineering recognised by All India Council for Technical Education
3. Age Limit for TNUSRB - Sub Inspector ( Technical )

Written Examination Pattern: Part I: LANGUAGE ELIGIBILITY TEST (Applicable only for specific state-level recruitments as notified by the respective State Public Service Commissions) Part II: MAIN WRITTEN EXAMINATION The Written Examination for Sub-Inspector of Police (Fingerprint) recruitment across India will be of graduate level, with the expectation of basic knowledge equivalent to Class XII standard across all subjects. The syllabus comprises the following parts: For Open Quota Part I: General Knowledge (For Open and Departmental Candidates)
  1. General Science
    2. Covers general understanding and awareness of scientific concepts including:
    • Physics, Chemistry, and Biology
    • Scientific instruments, inventions, and discoveries
    • Scientists and their contributions
    • Human physiology, diseases, nutrition, genetics
    • Ecology, environmental awareness, biodiversity
    • Concepts of matter, motion, Newton’s laws, electricity, acids, bases, salts, etc.,
    • Awareness of national scientific institutions and laboratories (All topics are of basic/intermediate level)
  1. History of India
    • Key dates and events from the Indus Valley Civilization to modern India
    • Major empires and movements including the Indian National Movement
    • Contribution of freedom fighters and reformers
    • Evolution of Indian administration post-independence
  1. Geography of India
    • Indian regions, climatic zones, monsoon, and natural disasters
    • Major crops, cropping patterns, and agricultural zones
    • Towns, cities, ports, rivers, forests, wildlife, and national parks
    • Population distribution and development indicators
    • Major industries and mineral resources
  1. Indian Polity
    • Constitution of India and its salient features
    • Citizenship, elections, structure of legislature, executive, and judiciary
    • Central and state governance
    • Panchayati Raj, Urban Local Bodies
    • Fundamental Rights, Duties, Centre-State relations, foreign policy basics
  1. Current Events
    • Recent developments in Science, Technology, Trade, Transport & Communication
    • National and International Awards
    • Literature, Cinema, Fine Arts (Dance, Drama, Painting, etc.)
    • Major sporting events and achievements
    • Prominent national and international organizations
    • Important books, authors, abbreviations, and recent appointments
    • India's relations with neighboring and global countries
    • Culture, Arts, and Heritage of India
Part II: Technical Subjects (For Open and Departmental Candidates)
  1. Electronic Devices and Circuits
    • Semiconductors: intrinsic & extrinsic
    • Diodes, Zener Diode, Rectifier
    • Bipolar Junction Transistor (BJT), FET, UJT
    • SCR, DIAC, TRIAC, MOSFET, IGBT
    • Opto-Electronic Devices
    • Wave shaping circuits, Amplifiers, Oscillators
    • Power devices: Inverters, Converters, Choppers
    • Programmable Logic Controllers (PLC), Distributed Control Systems (DCS)
  1. Electrical Circuits and Machines
    • DC & AC Circuits: current, power, resistance, ohm’s and Kirchhoff’s laws
    • Resonance, Transformers, Power Supplies
    • Measuring Instruments: CRO, Digital Oscilloscope, DVM, DMM
    • Electrical Machines: Generators, Induction motors, Universal motors, Stepper motors
    • Transducers, Sensors, Trigger circuits, Test instruments
  1. Microelectronics and Microprocessor Applications
    • Number systems, Boolean Algebra, Logic Gates
    • Combinational & Sequential Circuits
    • Operational Amplifiers, Phase Locked Loops
    • ADC & DAC Converters
    • Microprocessor 8085 & Microcontroller 8051
    • Architecture, Instruction Set, Programming, Interfacing
    • Serial communication and interrupt handling
  1. Network, Antenna, and Propagation
    • Networks, Equalizers, Attenuators, Filters
    • Antennas: Dipoles, Yagi-Uda, Helical, Log-periodic, Slot, Spiral, and Array Antennas
    • Parameters and measurements
    • Radio wave propagation
  1. Communication Engineering and Systems
    • Analog Modulation: AM, FM
    • Digital Communication: PCM, DPCM, Modulation techniques
    • Information theory, Channel coding, Spread spectrum
    • Optical Communication
    • Wireless Technologies: 2G, 3G, 4G, Cellular concepts
    • Radar & Navigation Systems
  1. Computer Applications and Programming
    • Computer fundamentals, Hardware/Software setup, troubleshooting
    • Peripherals: Printers, Scanners, AV Systems, Fax
    • Operating software: Word Processing, Spreadsheet, PowerPoint, Internet tools
    • Networking basics: OSI, TCP/IP, 802.x Protocols
    • Cybersecurity: Cryptography, Authentication, Integrity, Encryption algorithms
  1. Programming in C
    • Introduction to C language
    • Operators, Control Structures, Input/Output functions
    • Basic C programs and applications
